The Five-Tier Policy Hierarchy

Every INCS policy document should occupy a defined tier. Tier determines authority, update process, audience, and what can reference it. A higher-tier document always prevails in a conflict. No policy content should exist in two documents at the same tier.

Core principle: Each policy domain has exactly one authoritative home. All other documents may reference that home — they may never reproduce it. A policy that lives in two places is a policy you'll have to update twice, and the second update will always be forgotten.

Board Governance Documents
Board-adopted. Highest authority. Establishes legal obligations, authorizer compliance, and school identity.
Policy Handbook (Revised)
The master compliance document satisfying Charter Commission LEA policy requirements. Contains all board-level policies: governance, ethics, grievance, records, student rights, discipline, special populations. All other documents exist beneath or beside this one.
Owner: Board + Head of Schools Review: Annual (Board) Public-facing

What Stands Alone — and Why

A document earns standalone status when it has a distinct legal mandate, a separate audience, a regulatory update cycle, or operational scope that doesn't belong nested inside another document. The test: would merging this into another document make it harder to find, harder to update, or harder to comply with?

Decision criteria for standalone status: The document has (1) a distinct legal or regulatory mandate requiring it to exist as its own artifact, (2) a defined audience that differs from the primary document's audience, (3) a separate update cadence driven by external regulation, or (4) content sensitive enough to require different access controls than the main document.

Standalone ✓

Has its own legal mandate OR distinct audience OR different update driver OR access control requirement. Would be harder to maintain if nested.

Policy Handbook, Financial Policies, Wellness Policy, EOP, Employee Handbook, Family Handbook, HACCP Plan

Appendix to a parent ↓

Supports a parent document's compliance. Too technical or specialized for the body of the document. Has no independent regulatory mandate — just contextual utility.

HACCP appendix to Wellness Policy, FERPA opt-out form, drill schedules, snack standards, background check policy detail

Retire or merge ✗

Duplicates content that exists authoritatively elsewhere. Was created to fill a gap that no longer exists. Adds maintenance burden without adding governance value.

Single-Source Rules — What Exists Only Once

These are the policy domains where the current document set has created costly duplication. For each domain, one document owns the rule. Every other document may reference it briefly — it may not restate, paraphrase into policy, or create exceptions. The reference should name the authoritative document explicitly.

Three compliance frameworks now govern INCS policy: In addition to the internal document hierarchy, three external frameworks create binding requirements that must be addressed across the document set. (1) TPCSC Subrecipient Monitoring Tool (revised 1/25/2026) — governs ESSA/IDEA program compliance, fiscal monitoring, personnel records, time-and-effort certifications, and whistleblower notifications. (2) TN Child Care Standards, Chapter 0520-12-01 (Feb 2024) — applies to INCS as a state-approved Montessori school program; governs staff age minimums (21+), pre-service orientation, annual training curriculum (13 required topics), medical fitness documentation, background check sequencing (before employment, not after), and posted compliance requirements. (3) Employee Handbook 2026-27 — the updated handbook now explicitly acknowledges DHS-regulated programming requirements, but several Child Care Standards requirements are not yet captured in any current document.
How to reference without reproducing: Instead of restating the reimbursement rules in the Employee Handbook, write: "Reimbursements are governed by the INCS Financial Policies & Procedures (Section: Employee Reimbursements). Employees must follow that policy when submitting any reimbursement request." One sentence. No duplication. No drift.

What Becomes an Appendix

Appendices are policy-supporting content that is too detailed or specialized for the policy body, but too important to exist separately. An appendix lives inside its parent document, is updated when the parent is updated, and shares the parent's access controls. Content becomes an appendix when it: (a) is referenced by the body, (b) would clutter the body, and (c) has no independent life outside that parent.

The appendix test: If a piece of content could be removed from a document and the document's core compliance would be unaffected, it's probably an appendix. If removing it would create a compliance gap, it belongs in the body. Forms, schedules, reference tables, and technical standards are almost always appendices.

Implementation Sequence

Architecture decisions are only as good as their execution. This sequence prioritizes compliance gaps and safety risks first, then resolves structural conflicts, then builds the long-term governance infrastructure. Updated June 2026 to reflect remediation progress across three document tracks.

Sequencing principle: Fix the things that create legal or safety exposure first. Structural elegance is a second-order concern. A school with a complete FERPA notice and a working EOP is in better shape than a school with a perfectly organized document hierarchy but missing signatures.

Child Care Standards (0520-12-01-.07(15)) require TBI/FBI background, Vulnerable Persons Registry, and Sex Offender Registry checks to be completed BEFORE an employee assumes duties. The current Employee Handbook allows reimbursement after start date and acknowledges that clearances may be pending after hire. Revise both the hiring procedure and handbook language to make pre-employment clearance unambiguous. Add a written hold process for cases where agency determinations are delayed.

Owner: Director of Operations · Before: Revised for all 2026-27 hires · Affects: Employee Handbook + hiring checklist
B

Create a staff pre-service orientation checklist (2-hour requirement)

Child Care Standards require every new employee (including substitutes and volunteers) to complete a 2-hour pre-service orientation before assuming duties covering: program philosophy, emergency procedures, discipline policies, child abuse reporting, SIDS, and child reception/dismissal. Create a documented checklist, assign it to the Director of Operations to administer, and add a completion log to the personnel file system.

Owner: Director of Operations · Before: August 1 · Affects: Employee Handbook (onboarding section), staff records
C

Document the annual staff training curriculum (13 required topics)

Child Care Standards require annual instruction within the first 2 weeks and each year after on 13 specific topics including child abuse detection, food allergies, shaken baby syndrome, abusive head trauma, safe sleep procedures, disease control, and developmentally appropriate practices. The current bloodborne pathogen training covers one item. Create an annual training calendar and documentation process. Add to the Employee Handbook's compliance training section. All training must be documented in personnel files and available for TDOE review.

Owner: Head of Schools + Director of Operations · Before: August 1 · Affects: Employee Handbook, personnel records
D

Add staff medical fitness documentation requirement

Child Care Standards require a written statement from a licensed physician, psychologist, or clinician verifying each staff member is physically capable of caring for children in a group setting. This must be on file within 10 calendar days of employment and renewed every 3 years. Add this to the Employee Handbook's onboarding requirements, to the hiring checklist, and to the personnel records documentation list. No current document references this requirement.

Owner: Director of Operations · Before: August 1 · Affects: Employee Handbook (Section 2 or onboarding), personnel file requirements
E

Add federal whistleblower written notification to Financial Policies

TPCSC fiscal monitoring (2 CFR § 200.217) requires employees to be notified in writing of whistleblower protections under 41 U.S.C. 4712 (federal fraud reporting). The 2026-27 Employee Handbook now cites Tennessee whistleblower law (T.C.A. § 50-1-304) but the federal statute is not referenced. The Financial Policies — the document governing federal grant use — must include a written employee notification provision citing 41 U.S.C. 4712. This is a direct TPCSC monitoring checklist item.

Owner: Director of Operations · Before: Next TPCSC self-assessment submission · Affects: Financial Policies
F

Establish semi-annual time-and-effort certification process

TPCSC fiscal monitoring requires semi-annual certifications for employees funded 100% by a single federal award, plus after-the-fact monthly distribution records for employees split-funded across multiple programs. Create a specific form and process, assign a responsible role (Director of Operations / back-office provider), set the certification dates (typically December and June), and document this in the Financial Policies. This is a recurring TPCSC monitoring finding when absent.

F

Correct PTO rollover language for 12-month employees — Employee Handbook Section 10

Current language states "PTO does not roll over. Any days not used or sold back are forfeited at year end" with no distinction between employee types. 12-month (calendar year) employees are allowed to carry over up to 5 days. Section 10 must be revised to make this distinction explicit: the no-rollover rule applies to 10-month school calendar employees; 12-month employees may carry over up to 5 days. Confirm whether the sell-back option (up to 3 days at $150/day) applies to 12-month employees as well or is limited to 10-month staff, and document accordingly.

Add 21-year staff age minimum to hiring criteria

Child Care Standards require all teachers, assistant teachers, directors, and assistant directors hired after June 30, 2017 to be at least 21 years of age. This hiring minimum is not documented anywhere in the current INCS policy set. Add it to the Employee Handbook's employment section and to any hiring checklist or job description templates. I

Clarify Vulnerable Persons Registry as a separate required check

The Child Care Standards require three distinct checks: TBI/FBI background, Vulnerable Persons Registry, and Sex Offender Registry — each has its own process, timeline, and re-check schedule (fingerprints must be re-taken every 5 years). The Employee Handbook and EOP Appendix I currently describe these as a unified "background check process." Revise both documents to explicitly enumerate all three checks, their re-check schedules, and the exclusionary criteria for the Vulnerable Persons Registry.

Owner: Director of Operations · Before: August 1 · Affects: Employee Handbook, EOP Appendix I
