The Student & Family Handbook cover page uses "Invictus Nashville Montessori School." This creates a branding and potential legal identity problem on the primary public-facing document.

Child Care Standards — Staff Medical Fitness Statement (0520-12-01-.05(f)(3))
Chapter 0520-12-01 (Child Care Standards) — not addressed in any document
Tennessee's Standards for School-Administered Child Care Programs require documented verification from a licensed physician, psychologist, clinician, nurse practitioner, or PA that each staff member is physically capable of safely caring for children in a group setting. This documentation must be on file within 10 calendar days of employment and renewed every 3 years. No current INCS document references this requirement. The Employee Handbook, EOP, and Policy Handbook are all silent on it.
High Priority
Child Care Standards — Mandatory Annual Training Curriculum (0520-12-01-.07(6))
Chapter 0520-12-01 — partially addressed in Employee Handbook (bloodborne pathogens only)
The Child Care Standards require annual instruction within the first 2 weeks on the job and each year after in 13 specific topics: child abuse detection/reporting/prevention, parent-center communication, disease control, certificate of approval requirements, infection risks to female employees of childbearing age, food allergies, supervision during high-risk activities (eating, outdoor play), meal service/safe food prep, shaken baby syndrome, abusive head trauma, safe sleep procedures, developmentally appropriate practices, and discipline/supervision policies. The Employee Handbook covers bloodborne pathogens and mandated reporting but does not document the full annual training curriculum, leaving a certification gap.
High Priority
Child Care Standards — Pre-Service Orientation (2-Hour Requirement Before Assuming Duties)
Chapter 0520-12-01-.07(5) — not addressed in any document
All new employees must complete a 2-hour orientation before assuming duties covering: program philosophy, emergency procedures, child discipline policies, child abuse reporting policies, SIDS, and child reception/dismissal procedures. This is a pre-start requirement. The Employee Handbook's onboarding references exist but do not document the specific 2-hour pre-service orientation as a compliance checkpoint. Substitutes and volunteers are also subject to orientation requirements.
High Priority
Child Care Standards — Background Check Timing Conflict (0520-12-01-.07(15))
Chapter 0520-12-01 vs. Employee Handbook 2026-27 (Section 4)
The Child Care Standards require TBI/FBI background checks, Vulnerable Persons Registry checks, and Sex Offender Registry checks to be obtained BEFORE employment/assuming duties. The Employee Handbook states that INCS will reimburse background check costs "through payroll following the employee's start date" and acknowledges that "some agency clearances may remain pending after an employee's start date." This creates a structural gap: employees may begin work before all required DHS clearances are complete, which violates the standard. Work restrictions pending clearance are acknowledged but the pre-employment requirement must be unambiguous.
High Priority

For employees hired after June 30, 2017, all teachers, assistant teachers, directors, and assistant directors must be at least 21 years of age. This requirement applies to staff in a child care program. No current INCS policy document acknowledges this hiring minimum. The Employee Handbook, Policy Handbook HR section, and EOP background check appendix are all silent on this age requirement. It must be added to hiring criteria and onboarding documentation.

Child Care Standards — Vulnerable Persons Registry Separate from Background Check
Chapter 0520-12-01-.07(15) — Employee Handbook conflates into one "background check" process
The Child Care Standards require three separate checks: (1) Department of Health Vulnerable Persons Abuse Registry, (2) TBI/FBI fingerprint and background check, and (3) TBI/FBI Sex Offender Registry. The Employee Handbook references "DHS-regulated programming" and multiple checks but describes them as one unified process. The EOP Appendix I references "background check" without distinguishing the Vulnerable Persons Registry as a separate required check. Each check has different timelines and re-check schedules (fingerprint renewal every 5 years per the Standards) — all must be separately documented in policy.
Medium Priority
Child Care Standards — Annual Certificate of Approval Posting Requirement
Chapter 0520-12-01-.05(9) — not referenced in any document
The current certificate of approval must be posted near the main entrance in a conspicuous location during operating hours. The DHS toll-free complaint number and DCS child abuse number must also be posted near the main entrance and at each telephone. No INCS policy document establishes responsibility for maintaining these postings, verifying they are current, or replacing them when renewed. This should be assigned to the Director of Operations in the EOP or Wellness Policy, and acknowledged as an annual operational task.
Low Priority
EOP Confidentiality Not Referenced in Any Other Document
Doc 8 (EOP) — no cross-references found
The EOP is explicitly confidential under T.C.A. 49-6-808 and exempt from public records requests. No other document in the set references this classification, establishes a distribution log, or tells staff how to handle a public records request that inadvertently seeks EOP materials. The Policy Handbook's public records section should include a note that the EOP is exempt.
